Policy: OnabotulinumtoxinA (Botox) for Chronic Migraine Prophylaxis\n\nCriteria for Approval:\n1. Patient is 18 years of age or older.\n2. Diagnosis of chronic migraine (>= 15 headache days per month for > 3 months, with >= 8 days featuring migraine characteristics).\n3. Failure, intolerance, or contraindication to at least two different classes of oral migraine prophylactic medications (e.g., beta-blockers, anticonvulsants, antidepressants).\n4. Request is for OnabotulinumtoxinA (Botox) specifically.\n\nRequired Information:\n- Patient age.\n- Confirmation of chronic migraine diagnosis (frequency/duration of headaches).\n- List of oral migraine prophylactic medications tried (name, dosage, duration, reason for stopping - ineffective/intolerable/contraindicated).\n- Medication being requested (must specify Botox or OnabotulinumtoxinA).
